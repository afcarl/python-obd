  38  """Description: 
  39   
  40  SpeedMeter Tries To Reproduce The Behavior Of Some Car Controls (But Not Only), 
  41  By Creating An "Angular" Control (Actually, Circular). I Remember To Have Seen 
  42  It Somewhere, And I Decided To Implement It In wxPython. 
  43   
  44  SpeedMeter Starts Its Construction From An Empty Bitmap, And It Uses Some 
  45  Functions Of The wx.DC Class To Create The Rounded Effects. Everything Is 
  46  Processed In The Draw() Method Of SpeedMeter Class. 
  47   
  48  This Implementation Allows You To Use Either Directly The wx.PaintDC, Or The 
  49  Better (For Me) Double Buffered Style With wx.BufferedPaintDC. The Double 
  50  Buffered Implementation Has Been Adapted From The wxPython Wiki Example: 
  51   
  52  http://wiki.wxpython.org/index.cgi/DoubleBufferedDrawing 
  53   
  54   
  55  Usage: 
  56   
  57  SpeedWindow1 = SM.SpeedMeter(parent, 
  58                               bufferedstyle, 
  59                               extrastyle, 
  60                               mousestyle 
  61                               ) 
  62   
  63  None Of The Options (A Part Of Parent Class) Are Strictly Required, If You 
  64  Use The Defaults You Get A Very Simple SpeedMeter. For The Full Listing Of 
  65  The Input Parameters, See The SpeedMeter __init__() Method. 
  66   
  67   
  68  Methods And Settings: 
  69   
  70  SpeedMeter Is Highly Customizable, And In Particular You Can Set: 
  71   
  72  - The Start And End Angle Of Existence For SpeedMeter; 
  73  - The Intervals In Which You Divide The SpeedMeter (Numerical Values); 
  74  - The Corresponding Thicks For The Intervals; 
  75  - The Interval Colours (Different Intervals May Have Different Filling Colours); 
  76  - The Ticks Font And Colour; 
  77  - The Background Colour (Outsize The SpeedMeter Region); 
  78  - The External Arc Colour; 
  79  - The Hand (Arrow) Colour; 
  80  - The Hand's Shadow Colour; 
  81  - The Hand's Style ("Arrow" Or "Hand"); 
  82  - The Partial Filler Colour; 
  83  - The Number Of Secondary (Intermediate) Ticks; 
  84  - The Direction Of Increasing Speed ("Advance" Or "Reverse"); 
  85  - The Text To Be Drawn In The Middle And Its Font; 
  86  - The Icon To Be Drawn In The Middle; 
  87  - The First And Second Gradient Colours (That Fills The SpeedMeter Control);
